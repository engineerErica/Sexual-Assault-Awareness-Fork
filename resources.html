You are not alone. We are here to help you.

Sexual Assault can happen to anyone, regardless of age or gender.

Majority of people that experience sexual assault are violated by someone they know. If you are wondering whether or not you experienced an act of sexual violence, know that any unwanted or uninvited act of a sexual nature is considered a form of sexual violence. For more information about what is considered assault, click here.

Needing support is not a weakness. In fact, asking for help is a sign of courage.

Regardless of the severity of an incident, there are people who want to listen to your story. No one deserves the trauma of sexual assault, but for those of us who have been assaulted, there are resources available to aid your healing. Linked below is the Rape, Abuse, & Incest National Network (RAINN), which has online chat support as well as a telephone hot-line: +1-800-656-HOPE (4673).

Trust your instincts. Listen to your intuition.

In a fight or flight situation, it's important to prioritize your safety and take action to defend yourself against any potential danger, especially in cases of sexual assault. Being proactive and prepared can make all the difference in such situations. Remember that there is no shame in defending yourself and that it's always better to err on the side of caution. If you feel uncomfortable or threatened, don't hesitate to create distance or leave the situation altogether. Being aware of your surroundings and having a plan in place can also help you to react quickly and effectively.
